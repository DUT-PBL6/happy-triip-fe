1.1 Definition of Terms

This Privacy Policy is based on the following terminology definitions:

Identifiable natural person is a person who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as name, identifier, location data, online identifier or one or more specific physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social factors of that natural person. Identity can also be provided by associating that information or other additional knowledge. This is independent of the appearance, form or physical embodiment of the information (photos, videos or audio recordings may also contain personal data).
  • “Processing” means any activity or set of activities performed on personal data or on a collection of personal data, whether or not by automated means (i.e., assisted by technology). This includes, in particular, collecting (i.e. procuring), recording, organizing, structuring, storing, adapting or changing, retrieving, consulting, using, disclosing by transmission upload, disseminate or make available, adapt or combine, restrict, erase or destroy personal data as well as change the definition of the objective or purpose for which it was originally used for processing. data handling.

  • “Processor” means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body that processes personal data on behalf of the controller, in particular in accordance with the instructions of such authority.
  • “Third party” means a natural or juridical person, public authority, agency or other entity that is not a data subject, controller, processor and persons, under the direct authority of the user. controller or processor, authorized to process personal data; this also includes other legal entities of the group.
  • “Consent” by data subject means any indication given freely, specifically, informed and unambiguously about the wishes of data subject which he or she, by a statement or by an express affirmative act, indicating consent to the processing of personal data concerning him or her.

Temporary cookies, also known as in-memory cookies or “session cookies”, are cookies that are deleted after you leave our website and close your browser. This allows for example to save the login status or any search terms that have been entered. We use such cookies, among other things, to measure reach or for marketing purposes. Persistent cookies are automatically deleted after a specific period of time, which may vary depending on the cookie. However, you can delete these cookies at any time in your browser's privacy settings.
